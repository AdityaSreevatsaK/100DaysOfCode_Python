Top 10 Movies of All Time

Movies are dreams in motion.

  1. The Godfather (1972)

    2. A masterclass in storytelling, character development, and cultural impact, this film redefined the crime genre and set a benchmark for filmmaking.

  2. The Shawshank Redemption (1994)

    3. An inspiring tale of hope, friendship, and resilience, this film has universal appeal with its emotionally resonant narrative.

  3. Schindler's List (1993)

    4. A harrowing and deeply moving portrayal of the Holocaust, this film is lauded for its historical importance and emotional depth.

  4. Pulp Fiction (1994)

    5. With its nonlinear storytelling, quirky characters, and iconic dialogue, it revolutionised modern cinema and inspired countless filmmakers.

  5. Forrest Gump (1994)

    6. A heartwarming journey through life's unpredictability, blending historical events with a deeply personal narrative about love and perseverance.

  6. The Godfather Part II (1974)

    7. An unparalleled sequel, praised for its parallel storytelling of the rise of Vito Corleone and the moral decline of Michael Corleone.

  7. The Good, the Bad and the Ugly (1966)

    8. A landmark in the Western genre, featuring stunning cinematography, a gripping plot, and an iconic score by Ennio Morricone.

  8. 12 Angry Men (1957)

    9. A brilliant examination of justice and human psychology, set entirely in one room, showcasing incredible dialogue and character dynamics.

  9. Casablanca (1942)

    10. Timeless romance and political intrigue, featuring unforgettable performances and iconic lines like "Here's looking at you, kid."

  10. Citizen Kane (1941)

    11. A revolutionary film in terms of narrative structure, cinematography, and storytelling, often called the greatest film ever made.

Note: This list reflects general opinions and is not a personal ranking.
